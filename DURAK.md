In Durak, everyone’s dealt cards, and one trump suit is revealed. Players take turns attacking the next player with one or more cards. The defender must beat them with higher cards of the same suit or trumps. If the defender succeeds, the attack cards are discarded; if not, the defender picks them up. Attacks rotate, and players draw back up to a set hand size until the deck runs out. The last player left holding cards is the “Durak” (the fool).

In “Perevodnoy Durak”—often translated as “Transfer Durak”—if the defender has a card of the same rank as the attacker’s card, instead of defending, they can “transfer” the attack to the next player. Now that next player must defend against the combined attack. This twist adds strategic complexity, as you can pass tricky situations along if you’ve got the right card!

Use 36 deck.
